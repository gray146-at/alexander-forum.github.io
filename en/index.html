Our Mission

The Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience is an independent, non-profit Think & Do Tank based in Vienna. We connect observation, strategy, and practice to open new perspectives on change, crises, and cohesion.

We see resilience as a shared task across politics, science, culture, and society. Societal and personal crises are intertwined; unresolved tensions weaken the whole — and those under pressure can contribute less.

A core focus of our work is how rules, institutions, and public narratives keep pace with reality. The fact that something is established does not automatically mean it still works well. We treat critique as part of democratic maintenance: precise, evidence-aware, and attentive to unintended consequences. Resilience is not only an individual capacity, but also a property of systems that remain willing to learn.

Principles & Quality Standards

To make complexity workable without flattening it, we follow a small set of guiding standards:

  • Context before reflex
    We ask not only what happens, but how people and systems reach their breaking points.
  • Responsibility without dehumanization
    Explanations do not replace boundaries, but they improve prevention.
  • Proportionality
    We weigh problems, measures, and side effects rather than relying on symbolic hardness.
  • Transparency
    We mark uncertainty instead of disguising it.
  • Interdisciplinarity
    Psychology, political science, medicine, and social theory are read together on purpose.
