Our Mission

The Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience is an independent, non-profit Think & Do Tank based in Vienna. We connect societal observation, strategic thinking and practical experience to open new perspectives on change, crises and cohesion.

We understand resilience as a shared task of politics, science, culture and society. Societal and personal crises are interwoven: Those under pressure can contribute less – and unresolved tensions weaken the whole.

Focus Areas

Our approach is interdisciplinary – from climate and conflicts to technology and security, psychology and medicine. We work openly, name uncertainties transparently and publish analyses, assessments and reports that provide orientation, make shortcomings visible and inspire solutions.

  • Resilience & societal stress tests
    Between adaptation, exhaustion and new beginnings.
  • Information & trust
    Communication, fear, polarization and responsibility in the digital age.
  • Technology & risk
    How progress changes stability and security.
  • Identity & cohesion
    Belonging, trauma and the question of what sustains a community.

Our Approach

We promote reflection over information overload and a dialogue that tolerates contradictions and takes different perspectives seriously. The Forum invites discourse and seeks ways for constructive thinking to become collective action.

Our work remains practice-oriented – in analyses and syntheses, in briefings for organizations and in workshops that enable exchange and strengthen resilience as a shared attitude.

Foundation Dossier: Resilience – Interdisciplinary Analysis and Future Competence

Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience (Vienna) · October 10, 2025 · Version 1.1

Resilience is the ability to cope with crises and grow from them – understood biologically, psychologically, socially and systemically. The dossier explains why resilience is part of our name and how it can be promoted practically: from childhood attachment to organizational culture, from neuroplasticity to community resilience – including digital resilience as a new cultural technique.

Why Resilience in the Title?

For us, resilience is a design principle: creating conditions under which people and communities don't just survive, but thrive.

  • – Dynamic concept: Resilience is learnable and shapeable.
  • – Biopsychosocial: Person × Environment – protective factors can overcompensate risks.
  • – Practice-oriented: Exercises, organizational transfer, community and system resilience.
  • – Digital: Information overload, social comparison & "Digital Resilience Literacy".

Our Alexandrian Namesakes

The name "Alexander" is our program – as an attitude, not a personality cult. The personalities gathered here – men and women with "Alexander" connections – show different facets of resilience and reflection: systemic thinking, innovative spirit, moral integrity, institutional stability and personal exploratory drive.

Alexander the Great

Strategy & Adaptability

Military commander and state founder with enormous impact – and ambivalences. His campaigns spread knowledge and connectivity, but were also violent. Lesson: Leadership in uncertainty requires speed and translation – but always with ethics checks.

  • Deciding under uncertainty
  • Cultural translation work
  • Reflecting power, limiting harm

Hypatia of Alexandria

Education as Lighthouse

Philosopher, mathematician and teacher in late ancient Alexandria. Hypatia stands for the idea that education and knowledge transmission create cultural resilience: critical thinking, dialogue capacity and passing on the craft of reason – even under pressure.

  • Education as cultural immune system
  • Spaces for criticism stabilize communities
  • Knowledge outlasts short-term storms

Alexandra David-Néel

Courage at the Margins

Traveler, author, Tibet researcher. She crossed borders – geographically, culturally, socially – and combined adventurousness with inner discipline. Her life path shows: Resilience emerges when curiosity, self-leadership and respect for the foreign come together.

  • Exploration strengthens self-efficacy
  • Inner order in uncertain environments
  • Cultural sensitivity as resource

Alexandra Kollontai

Shaping Solidarity

Politician, feminist, diplomat – one of the first women in high state offices. Kollontai fought for social security, women's rights and institutional reforms. She represents the insight: Collective resilience grows when structures enable fairness.

  • Resilience needs institutions & networks
  • Justice is prevention
  • Persistence against resistance

Alexander Solzhenitsyn

Truth as Resistance

Camp survivor and author whose work focuses on witnessing, meaning and responsibility. He reminds us: Psychological resilience after trauma requires truthfulness – personally and societally.

  • Memory protects against repetition
  • Meaning-orientation stabilizes
  • Witnessing creates culture of responsibility

Alexander Fleming

Errors as Goldmine

Discoverer of penicillin. His openness to the unexpected – and a contaminated Petri dish – became a medical breakthrough. Resilience here means: error culture, observational acuity, rapid translation.

  • Making serendipity productive
  • Error-friendly research culture
  • Connection of lab & practice

Alexander von Humboldt

Making Connections Visible

Natural scientist and systems thinker. He connected ecology, climate, data and culture – locally observed, globally understood. His lesson: Resilience begins with seeing interdependencies and long-term horizons.

  • System understanding as foundation
  • Interdisciplinarity increases adaptability
  • Think long-term, act short-term
