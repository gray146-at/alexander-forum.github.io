Our Mission

The Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience is an independent, non-profit Think & Do Tank based in Vienna. We combine societal observation, strategic thinking and practical experience to open new perspectives on change, crises and social cohesion.

We understand resilience as a shared responsibility of politics, science, culture and society. Societal and personal crises are intertwined: Those under pressure can contribute less – and unresolved tensions weaken the whole.

Core Dossier: Resilience – Interdisciplinary Analysis and Future Competence

Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience (Vienna) · October 10, 2025 · Version 1.1

Resilience is the ability to overcome crises and grow from them – understood biologically, psychologically, socially and systemically. This dossier explains why resilience is part of our name and how it can be practically fostered: from childhood bonding to organizational culture, from neuroplasticity to community resilience – including digital resilience as a new cultural technique.

Why Resilience in the Name?

For us, resilience is a design principle: creating conditions under which people and communities don't just endure, but thrive.

  • – Dynamic concept: Resilience can be learned and shaped.
  • – Biopsychosocial: Person × Environment – protective factors can overcompensate risks.
  • – Practice-oriented: Exercises, organizational transfer, community and system resilience.
  • – Digital: Information overload, social comparison & "Digital Resilience Literacy".

Why "Alexander"?

The name is our program – as an attitude, not personality cult. We look for patterns of resilience, reflection and responsibility in historical lives.

Alexander the Great

Strategy & Adaptability

Military commander and state founder with enormous impact – and ambivalences. His campaigns spread knowledge and connectivity, but were also violent. Lesson: Leadership under uncertainty requires speed and translation – but always with ethical checks.

Hypatia of Alexandria

Education as Lighthouse

Philosopher, mathematician and teacher in late antique Alexandria. Hypatia stands for the idea that education and knowledge transfer create cultural resilience: critical thinking, dialogue capacity and passing on the craft of reason – even under pressure.
