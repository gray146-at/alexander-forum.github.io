Our Mission

The Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience is an independent, non-profit think & do tank based in Vienna. We connect societal observation, strategic thinking, and practical experience to open new perspectives on change, crises, and social cohesion.

We see resilience as a shared task across politics, science, culture, and society. Societal and personal crises are interwoven: under pressure, people contribute less – and unresolved tensions weaken the whole.

Topics

Our approach is interdisciplinary – from climate and conflicts to technology and security, as well as psychology and medicine. We are transparent about uncertainty and publish analyses, context pieces, and reports that provide orientation, surface blind spots, and inspire solutions.

  • Resilience & societal stress
    Between adaptation, exhaustion, and renewal.
  • Information & trust
    Communication, fear, polarization, and responsibility in the digital age.
  • Technology & risk
    How progress reshapes stability and security.
  • Identity & cohesion
    Belonging, trauma, and what holds a community together.

Approach

We promote reflection over overload and a dialogue that tolerates tensions while taking different perspectives seriously. We look for ways to turn constructive thinking into collective action.

We stay practical – through analyses and syntheses, through briefings for organizations, and through workshops that enable exchange and strengthen resilience as a shared stance.
