Our Mission

The Alexander Forum for Reflection & Resilience is an independent, non-profit Think & Do Tank based in Vienna. We combine societal observation, strategic thinking, and practical experience to open new perspectives on change, crises, and cohesion.

We understand resilience as a shared responsibility across politics, science, culture, and society. Societal and personal crises are intertwined: Those under pressure can contribute less – and unresolved tensions weaken the whole.

Fields of Work

Our approach is interdisciplinary – from climate and conflict to technology and security, as well as psychology and medicine. We work transparently, acknowledge uncertainty, and publish analyses, contextual assessments, and reports that provide orientation, reveal shortcomings, and inspire solutions.

  • Resilience & Societal Strain
    Between adaptation, exhaustion, and renewal.
  • Information & Trust
    Communication, fear, polarization, and responsibility in the digital age.
  • Technology & Risk
    How progress transforms stability and security.
  • Identity & Cohesion
    Belonging, trauma, and the question of what holds a community together.

Approach

We promote reflection over overstimulation and dialogue that tolerates contradictions and values diverse perspectives. The Forum invites open discourse and explores ways for constructive thinking to turn into collective action.

Our work remains practical – through analyses and syntheses, briefings for organizations and workshops that enable exchange and strengthen resilience as a shared mindset.
